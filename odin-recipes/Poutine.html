Poutine

Canadian Cuisine

Poutine is a classic Canadian comfort food that started in Quebec and has since become famous around the world. At its core, it’s a simple dish made with crispy fries, squeaky cheese curds, and hot gravy poured over top. The contrast between crunchy, creamy, and savory is what makes it so satisfying. Even though it’s simple, getting the balance just right is what turns good poutine into great poutine Over time, poutine has evolved far beyond its humble beginnings. You can now find versions topped with pulled pork, mushrooms, smoked meat, or even butter chicken. Some people see it as late-night food, while others treat it as a serious culinary dish. Either way, poutine is a perfect example of how comfort food can become part of a country’s identity

Ingredients

  • French Fries
  • Cheese Curds or Cheese
  • Gravy

How to Make

  1. Wash, peel (optional), and cut the potatoes into thick fries, then soak them in cold water for 30 minutes to remove excess starch.
  2. Heat oil and fry the potatoes until lightly golden, remove, and let them rest for a few minutes.
  3. Fry the potatoes a second time until crispy and deep golden brown, then season with salt.
  4. Prepare and heat the gravy so it’s hot and ready to pour.
  5. Place the fries on a plate, scatter cheese curds over them, and immediately pour hot gravy on top so the curds soften slightly
